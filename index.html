College Election Rules

1. Eligibility

To participate in the college election, you must meet the following eligibility criteria:

  • Be a currently enrolled student at the college.
  • Have a valid student ID.
  • Meet any additional requirements set by the college administration.

2. Candidacy

If you wish to run for a position in the college election, you must:

  • Submit your nomination within the specified deadline.
  • Provide all necessary information, including your name, student ID, and the position you are running for.
  • Abide by the campaign rules and regulations set by the college.

3. Campaigning

During the campaign period, candidates must adhere to the following guidelines:

  • Respect the college's code of conduct and maintain a positive and respectful campaign environment.
  • Avoid spreading false information or engaging in personal attacks against other candidates.
  • Comply with any restrictions on campaign materials, such as posters, banners, or online advertisements.

4. Voting

When casting your vote in the college election, remember the following:

  • Only currently enrolled students are eligible to vote.
  • Each student is allowed to vote once.
  • Follow the specified voting procedure and use the designated voting method (e.g., online voting, paper ballot).
  • Respect the privacy of other voters and maintain the integrity of the voting process.

5. Election Results

The election results will be announced on a specified date and made available to all students.

In case of any disputes or concerns regarding the election process, students can reach out to the election committee or the college administration for clarification or resolution.
